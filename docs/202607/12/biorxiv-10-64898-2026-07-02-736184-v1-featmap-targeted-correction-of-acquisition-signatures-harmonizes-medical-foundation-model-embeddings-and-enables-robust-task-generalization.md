## 摘要
医学基础模型将生物医学数据压缩为嵌入，支持多种下游临床任务。然而，模型部署受到外部数据性能下降的阻碍。研究表明，嵌入除了捕获生物学信息外，还捕获了采集特征（如硬件和技术差异）。有效的统一必须在不丢失生物信号的前提下移除采集特征，而现有方法未能充分平衡这种权衡。输入级归一化无法从嵌入中消除采集特征，而嵌入级方法以非目标性方式调整特征。我们提出FEATMAP，一种将采集特征建模为相似排列嵌入流形之间几何畸变的统一方法。利用隔离采集特征影响的配对数据，FEATMAP为每个基础模型拟合单个全局仿射变换，直接在嵌入空间中校正采集特征。这种目标性、可复用的校正旨在保留生物学和人口统计学变异，同时统一不同采集特征。在数字病理学的扫描仪和基础模型统一以及脑MRI的场强统一中，FEATMAP提高了跨条件嵌入相似性，无需重新训练即可减少性能差距，并显示出对齐不同嵌入空间的潜力。

## Abstract
AO_SCPLOWBSTRACTC_SCPLOWMedical foundation models compress biomedical data into embeddings that support diverse downstream clinical tasks. However, successful model deployment is hampered by performance degradation on external data. It is recognized that embeddings capture acquisition signatures, such as hardware and technical differences, in addition to biology. Effective harmonization must remove the acquisition signature while preserving biological signals, a trade-off that current methods fail to balance adequately. Input-level normalization fails to eliminate acquisition signatures from embeddings, whereas embedding-level methods adjust features in an untargeted manner. We present FEATMAP, a harmonization approach that models acquisition signatures as geometric distortions between manifolds of similarly arranged embeddings. Using paired data that isolate the effect of acquisition signatures, FEATMAP fits a single global affine transformation per foundation model to correct acquisition signatures directly in the embedding space. This targeted, reusable correction aims to preserve biological and demographic variation while harmonizing across acquisition signatures. Across scanner and foundation-model harmonization in digital pathology and field-strength harmonization in brain MRI, FEATMAP improves cross-condition embedding similarity, reduces performance gaps without retraining, and suggests potential for the alignment of disparate embedding spaces.
