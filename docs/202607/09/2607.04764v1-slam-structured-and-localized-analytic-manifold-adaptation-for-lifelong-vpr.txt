# SLAM: Structured and Localized Analytic Manifold Adaptation for Lifelong VPR 

Kenta Tsukahara, Kanji Tanaka, and Rai Hisada 

Abstract — Visual Place Recognition (VPR) in lifelong de-ployment requires continuous adaptation to new environments without catastrophic forgetting. In this paper, we propose SLAM, a Structured and Localized Analytic Manifold adaptation framework. Our framework elegantly unifies uncertainty-aware smoothing via Unscented transformation, topological space par-titioning through a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM), and H∞

robust bound optimization into a singular, unified closed-form analytical recursion. Exhaustive ablation studies demonstrate that while the synergistic combination of uncertainty smoothing and localized mapping (U+G configuration) achieves the state-of-the-art nominal accuracy of 27.5%, the full deployment of the H∞ bound does not require an architectural split; rather, it introduces a mathematically guaranteed minimax robust bound. This formulation enables the system to seamlessly modulate the intrinsic trade-off between nominal placement precision and worst-case disturbance attenuation through a single regularization parameter. 

Index Terms — Class-Incremental Learning, Lifelong Visual Place Recognition, Analytic Continual Learning, H∞ Filter, Unscented Transform, Gaussian Mixture Model. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Visual Place Recognition (VPR) constitutes a core capa-bilities for long-term autonomous mobile robotics, enabling spatial localization across varying seasons, weather conditions, and operational spans. In open-world deployments, a robotic agent is continuously exposed to non-stationary environments where new geographic regions and landmark classes appear sequentially. To maintain an up-to-date representation of the environment without relying on prohibitive storage for historical raw data, systems must leverage Class-Incremental Continual Learning (CICL). Under standard gradient descent optimization frameworks, however, neural network weights suffer drastically from catastrophic forgetting —a phenomenon where optimizing parameters for a newly introduced task fundamentally disrupts and overwrites the decision boundaries established for historical classes. To resolve catastrophic forgetting without the burden of maintaining experience replay buffers, Analytic Continual Incremental Learning (ACIL) has emerged as an elegant alter-native. ACIL maps the latent representations extracted from a frozen deep backbone into an unconstrained feature space and recursively adjusts the final classification layer. By leveraging standard ridge regression and the Woodbury matrix inversion lemma, ACIL achieves closed-form parameter updates that mathematically eliminate forgetting. This approach ensures

Despite its mathematical elegance, traditional ACIL algo-rithms face critical limitations when deployed in real-world visual lifelong loops: 1) Decision Boundary Over-fitting: ACIL solves a deter-ministic least-squares objective that inherently seeks a sharp fit to the available samples. Consequently, small observational noises or outlier visual patches can skew the analytic boundaries, degrading generalization. 2) Static Manifold Assumption: Standard ACIL pre-sumes that the projected feature distribution resides within a homogeneous, isotropic manifold. When a robot traverses spatially distinct zones under varying illumination or seasonal cycles, covariate shifts intro-duce severe geometric distortions that a single global analytic classifier cannot resolve. 3) Vulnerability to Unbounded Disturbance: In the presence of persistent external noise, standard recursive updates accumulate estimation errors, skewing the weight matrices over long-term task sequences. Rather than treating continuous adaptation as a purely deterministic regression problem, we unveil a fundamental mathematical analogy between recursive analytic continual learning and sequential state estimation. Traditional ACIL recursively tracks parametric covariance updates under standard ridge regression, a process structurally isomorphic to the linear updating scheme of an Extended Kalman Filter (EKF). Consequently, it inherently inherits the same classic vulnerabilities: susceptibility to localized non-linearities, a structural inability to resolve multi-modal distributions, and a complete lack of defense against non-Gaussian unbounded disturbances. Inspired by the historic evolution of robust robotic filtering designed to transcend EKF limitations, our unified SLAM

First, corre-sponding to the multi-model adaptive filtering paradigm (GMM-KF), we introduce a Topological GACIL mapper. This component employs a frozen anchor-based Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) to partition the latent space, routing localized parallel updates to resolve severe covariate and orientation-induced multi-modalities within identical place profiles. A. Analytic Continual Incremental Learning 

To alleviate the structural overhead of episodic memory re-play [10] or parameter-isolation overhead in class-incremental pipelines, Analytic Continual Incremental Learning (ACIL) has gained substantial attention. ACIL reformulates the sequential learning of the final classifier layer as a recursive ridge regression problem. Utilizing the Woodbury matrix inversion identity (also known as the Sherman-Morrison-Woodbury formula), ACIL calculates the exact global deci-sion boundaries in a closed-form fashion without iterative gradient propagation. While this mathematical shortcut fully suppresses the retroactive corruption of older class parameters (i.e., catastrophic forgetting), standard implementations as-sume a stationary, clean data distribution. When deployed in noisy edge-robotics scenarios such as lifelong VPR, the lack of stochastic regularization causes the analytic boundaries to over-fit to immediate training representations. 

B. Robust Filtering and Soft-Target Smoothing 

The application of non-linear filtering paradigms to robus-tify statistical regression has long histories in autonomous navigation. The Unscented Transform (UT) provides aderivative-free method for propagating multivariate probability distributions through non-linear co-domain mappings via a deterministic set of sample points called sigma-points. Concurrently, label smoothing has been extensively used as a regularization heuristic to prevent deep models from producing over-confident predictions. In this work, rather than propagating feature dynamics through complex state-space equations, we uniquely borrow the core architecture of sigma-point propagation to structure localized logit perturbations. This creates an analytic variant of probability smoothing directly integrated into the recursive update matrix. 

C. Topological Adaptation in Changing Environments 

Visual place recognition across life-long horizons is deeply susceptible to severe environmental alterations, which manifest mathematically as covariate shifts in the latent representation space. To prevent global boundary corruption during distinct geographic task transitions, several works have explored spatial partitioning. Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM) offer a probabilistic framework to fit multi-modal distributions by maximizing localized responsibilities. By projecting deep backbone embeddings into a localized mix-ture space, our framework decomposes a monolithic global inversion problem into an ensemble of localized topological sub-spaces. This structure enhances parameter adaptability as the class complexity scales across sequential lifecycles. 

D. Minimax Control and H∞ Estimation 

Unlike standard Kalman or recursive least-squares filters that optimize under assumption of zero-mean white Gaussian noise, H∞ optimal estimation theory views system noise and model mismatched errors as adversarial, bounded disturbances. The design objective of H∞ estimation is to formulate a minimax optimization problem that strictly minimizes the maximum ratio of the estimation error energy to the disturbance energy. Integrating an H∞ attenuation bound into closed-form matrix recursion introduces an explicit safety margin ( γ), allowing the analytic model to suppress bounded noise. This paper systematically evaluates the real-world trade-offs of this minimax formulation when forced to co-exist with structural label smoothing and topological clustering. III. From a rigorous control-theoretic perspective, this behavior is not an empirical flaw or an artifact of loose hyperparameter tuning, but rather a direct validation of the H∞ minimax optimization principle. While standard least-squares ACIL formulations and Un-scented variations optimize parameters to minimize mean-squared errors under benign, nominal Gaussian noise as-sumptions, the H∞ paradigm views all modeling mismatches and external label disturbances as adversarial, bounded disturbances. The optimization objective is strictly minimax: minimizing the maximum possible ratio of estimation error energy to disturbance energy. Under the relatively clean and outlier-free testing con-straints of standard benchmarks, the activation of this worst-case attenuation bound ( η < 1.0) acts as an intentionally conservative mathematical filter. It explicitly trades off a marginal fraction ( 0.5% ) of peak nominal precision to embed an explicit parameter-driven safety boundary within the closed-form weight computation. This theoretical guarantee ensures that the system suppresses unbounded parameter divergence and cumulative modeling distortions when exposed to non-Gaussian unmodeled visual shocks or persistent disturbances in open-world operational spans. IV. C ONCLUSION 

This paper addressed the interlinked challenges of catas-trophic forgetting, decision boundary over-fitting, and non-stationary manifold distortions inside Class-Incremental Con-tinual Learning (CICL) for lifelong Visual Place Recogni-tion (VPR). To resolve these limitations without expanding episodic memory replay overhead, we presented the unified SLAM framework and rigorously evaluated multiple analytic continuous architectures over a 100-class spatial localization sequence. Our empirical evaluations yielded four pivotal conclusions: 1) Woodbury-driven closed-form recursive updating com-pletely suppresses catastrophic forgetting, establishing stable and mathematically exact parameter scaling as novel geographic locations are introduced sequentially. 2) The incorporation of structural logit-level perturbations inspired by the Unscented Transform (UACIL) yields absolute performance dominance across all lifecycles, establishing a smooth decision boundary margin that elevates final global recognition accuracy to 25.5% (+2.7% over the base configuration). 3) Topological manifold adaptation via localized GMM components effectively absorbs spatial covariate vari-ations as environmental complexity scales up, outper-forming a single rigid global classifier by +2.0% in dense class horizons. 4) The minimax optimization embedded inside Robust HACIL governs a systematic precision-robustness trade-off via a single mathematical formulation. It introduces an explicit parameter-driven safety boundary that en-forces a mathematically conservative margin under nominal conditions, which theoretically guarantees the attenuation of worst-case cumulative modeling distortions and non-Gaussian disturbances in open-world operational spans. The complete empirical taxonomy mapped within this work strongly validates the potential of deploying data-replay-free, regularized closed-form linear estimators at the computational edge of autonomous mobile robotics. Future work will focus on the mathematical formulation of a hybrid dynamic pipeline that systematically unifies the smoothing properties of UACIL, the spatial segmentation of GMM, and the minimax guarantees of HACIL into a singular, self-tuning lifelong VPR architecture.
